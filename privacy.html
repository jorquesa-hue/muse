Privacy notice

Last updated: 2026-07-16

Muse is a working prototype. Almost everything it does runs entirely in your browser, offline, against a dataset shipped with the app — nothing about a normal search leaves your device. This page covers the two things that do: the anonymous device id, and the two features that log data to make the app better for everyone.

What's collected

  • An anonymous device id (muse-sid). Generated randomly on your first visit and stored in your browser's local storage. It isn't tied to your name, email, or any account — there is no account. It exists only to group your own ratings together so the "good match / off" feedback stays consistent per device.
  • "Find anything" live searches. When you search for a title that isn't already in the offline catalog and the app finds it live, the resolved title, its category, and the derived matching data (genre, mood profile, themes, popularity — no personal data) are logged so a daily job can fold it into tomorrow's catalog for everyone.
  • Match ratings. If you tap "Spot on" / "Off" on a match, that rating, the device id, and the match context (which two works, which category, the score) are logged. This is the only feedback signal used to improve the matching algorithm over time.

What's NOT collected

No name, email, account, location, or advertising identifiers. No cross-site tracking. The live "find anything" feature does talk directly to Wikipedia and Wikidata from your browser to resolve a title — those requests go straight from your device to them, not through us, and their own privacy policies apply to that traffic.

Where it's stored

In a Supabase-hosted database. Write access uses a public, insert-only key by design — that key cannot read other people's data back out.

Retention

Logged searches and ratings are kept for up to 180 days, then deleted on a rolling basis.

Deletion

Since there's no account, we can't look up "your" data by name — but if you want your device's history erased sooner, clear muse-sid from your browser's local storage (this disconnects future ratings from past ones immediately) and email jorquesa@gmail.com with the approximate date/time and what you searched, and it will be removed on request.

Changes

This is a prototype and this notice may change as the app does. Meaningful changes will update the date above.
